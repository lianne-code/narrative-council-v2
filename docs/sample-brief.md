NARRATIVE BRIEF
Locked: April 2026
Owner: Sarah Chen, VP Marketing

COMMITTED CLAIM
B2B marketing teams that run their briefs through structured adversarial review ship campaigns that survive two fewer revision rounds — because the brief absorbs the conflict instead of the execution.

AUDIENCE
Senior marketing leaders (Director and above) at B2B SaaS companies who have shipped campaigns that performed below projection and attributed the miss to "creative execution" — when the real failure was an uncommitted brief that accumulated Safety Tax through every revision round.

PROOF
Internal analysis of 40 campaign post-mortems across enterprise marketing teams showed that campaigns with a named single owner and a locked brief required an average of 2.1 fewer revision rounds and had 34% higher message consistency scores between brief and final execution.

DILUTION RISK
The claim that revision rounds are a brief problem, not a creative problem, will be challenged by every creative director in the review. It should be defended. The data supports it. The instinct to soften it toward "briefs and creative both matter" is the Safety Tax asking for its payment.

NARRATIVE COMMIT POINT
The core claim — revision rounds are a brief problem — is locked. Post-commit changes require new evidence, not new stakeholder comfort.

CHAIRMAN'S NOTE
This brief carries a structural tension into execution: it indicts the review process that the people reading the final output run. The draft that performs best will not soften this tension — it will name it and make the reader feel it before offering the resolution.
